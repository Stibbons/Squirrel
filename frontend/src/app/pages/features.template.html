Meet Squirrel

What is Squirrel?

Squirrel is your swissknife of your investment life. It is an online tool gathering everything you need to find, decide, follow your investment.

But there plenty of similar tool online?

Most of the online portfolio managers offer you access to an automated investment strategy, with no effort from your side. Enjoy the best algorithms that make wonders, in the finance, work for you. Squirrel does not make these promises. What it does promise is to give you the best tool to track you portfolio and find your own strategy.

What makes it different from other online portfolio managers?

Before everything else, Squirrel is a tool aimed at guiding you from day 1 when your start you investment journey, to build step by step your own portfolio. You have all the meaningful information in front of you, in your Squirrel Dashboard.

There is no mystic algorithm behind Squirrel, no Award winning Trading Software that will make you rich. Just well crafted tools, specially targeted to private investors.

Squirrel is truly independent: no hidden fees, no commission on any trade. We do not sell your data. We don't ask for your mobile number because we do not want to sell you anything. We do not disturb you with annoying ads. We are not linked with any broker or bank.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have Questions about Squirrel?

General

A quite ambitious project coming from a modest idea born early 2014.

Hi! My name is Gaetan Semet. I am Software engineer who started a very humble investor life in 2014 and was very disappointed with existing tools to follow my different portfolios.

I opened several accounts on several broker, to exploit the best of each contract. Each contract has its own avdantages, and drawbacks. For example, my first stocks broker was quite good for stocks from my home country (France), but was over expensive for stocks abroad. I then opened another account on a cheaper broker of US stocks.

I also wanted to follow the Mutual Funds I had on contracts opened by my company. I wanted to tracke them like I would do with stocks.

Like many people, I started tracking my different stocks and mutual funds using a speadsheet software. But I felt very restricted when using such software, without beneficting from modern tools, dynamic and interactives charts such as what we can see on the web. If services like Dropbox allow you to have access to you file while on the go, you still need the software to open and check it.

So I started using Google Spreadsheet to follow my investments, and I felt in love with its the 100% online nature. No matter which day of the week we are, if I am at home or on the go, I could see and edit my portfolio instantanously.

But when data start to becomes quite heavy, with lot of custom import scripts, the Speadsheet started slowing down and become less confortable to work with. Ultimately, I was not able to open it on my mobile phone, even the tablet crashed.

I was also disappointed by the graphs themselves, they were not customable enough and not so good looking, compared to graph produced by JavaScript libraries such as HighCharts. Like for example:

Squirrel started as a personal work but I wanted to do it the right way, with cutting edge web technologies. And though maybe one day I could release it as a real service.

I took several months of hard evening work, first to learn web technologies and to build this web site.

Squirrel is built in Angular and Python.

As a reminder, Squirrel is able to follow the price evolution of almost any kind of Stocks and Mutual Fonds valorisation or Bonds. So, you only have to manually enter price of funds that are not publically available.

Unfortunately, Squirrel cannot retrieve automatically the movement you execute on your different accounts, such as buying a Stock or selling a mutual fund share. The first step would be to sign a contract with every broker, connect, and retrieve this information. It is a large number of contract to sign, and lot of these brokers and banks do not want, or do not have the culture to share this information. Interoperability is not a common word in the financial world.

The major issue to deal with is security. "Reading" data from your account means it is also possible to send orders. This would requires to store your credential on Squirrel server, and this is definitely not a good idea. Even with all means taken to protect all these sensitives information, such as "bank level cryptography", this would break the golden rule "Don't put all your eggs in the same basket", since all your passwords are at a single place. There is no simple say to garantee a total security in this sector, at one point all your password will need to be decrypted before being sent to your broker. We can setup what we call a "Two way authentication" technic, but this would not allow automatically, transparent update. This is the only way to avoid Squirrel to ever "know" your password at one point in the system.

The only solution that I can imagine is to cypher your credentials with the highest level of possible cryptography, that cannot be ever decyphered by the Squirrel server. The web interface would requires your to enter the passphrase and connect itself to your different brokers. In other word, I would be like if you would manually retrieve these data from your browser. Maybe one day this will be real!

Until this moment, unfortunately, you have to manually update your operations. But don't be affraid, Squirrel is pretty confortable and this is quite easy to do.
